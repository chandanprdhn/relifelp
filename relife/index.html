Frequently Asked Questions

Relife Hospital's palliative care program is distinguished by its three unique qualities: immediate access to critical care through a 24/7 on-site ICU, a team of palliative care certified providers including doctors, nurses, social workers, and spiritual counselors, and peaceful, private hospital rooms designed to provide a home-like environment.

The 24/7 on-site ICU at Relife Hospital ensures that patients have immediate access to critical care, so no time is lost during emergencies. This allows the palliative care team to rapidly respond and provide the necessary support to maintain patient comfort and well-being.

Relife Hospital's palliative care team, including doctors, nurses, social workers, and spiritual counselors, are all specially trained and certified in palliative care. This ensures they are equipped to provide the highest level of end-of-life support and personalized care to patients and their families.

Relife Hospital's hospital rooms are designed to feel like a peaceful, private home, rather than a traditional hospital setting. This home-like environment, with features such as large windows and personal touches, helps create a comforting and dignified atmosphere for patients receiving palliative care.

By having a dedicated team of palliative care certified providers, Relife Hospital can ensure that patients receive seamless, consistent care throughout their healthcare journey. This allows the team to develop a deep understanding of the patient's needs and preferences, resulting in more personalized and effective care.

In addition to the peaceful, private hospital rooms, Relife Hospital offers amenities such as on-site therapy gyms, quiet meditation rooms, and peaceful gardens. These features help create a soothing and restorative environment for palliative care patients and their families.

Relife Hospital's palliative care team, including social workers and spiritual counselors, is specifically trained to provide emotional support and counseling to patients and their families. This helps them navigate the challenges of a serious illness and cope with the end-of-life process.
